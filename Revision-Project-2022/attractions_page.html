- Indoor Activites -

VMFA

Cost: Free! (Just don't look at the giftshop)

Who is it for?: Probably not kids, unless you're going to a workshop event.

The Virginia Museum of Fine Arts, or VMFA, is an art museum in Richmond, Virginia, United States, which opened in 1936. The museum is owned and operated by the Commonwealth of Virginia.

Science Museum of Virginia

Cost: $$

Who is it for?: Anyone looking to learn something new. Kid friendly.

The Science Museum of Virginia is a science museum located in Richmond, Virginia. Established in 1970, it is an agency of the Commonwealth of Virginia. It is housed in the former Broad Street Station, built in 1917.

The Poe Museum

Cost: $

Who is it for?: The edgy teen that just read "Tell Tale Heart" for the first time.

The Poe Museum or the Edgar Allan Poe Museum, is a museum located in the Shockoe Bottom neighborhood of Richmond, Virginia, United States, dedicated to American writer Edgar Allan Poe. Though Poe never lived in the building, it serves to commemorate his time living in Richmond.

- Outdoor Activities -

Maymont

Cost: Free!

Who is it for?: Everyone! No dogs allowed though :C

Maymont is a 100-acre Victorian estate and public park in Richmond, Virginia. It contains Maymont Mansion, now a historic house museum, an arboretum, formal gardens, a carriage collection, native wildlife exhibits, a nature center, and Children's Farm.

Lewis Ginter

Cost: $$

Who is it for?: Plant lovers

Lewis Ginter Botanical Garden is a 50 acres, botanical garden in Henrico County, just outside of Richmond, Virginia. It features a conservatory, library cafe and tea house restaurant.

Belle Isle

Cost: Free!

Who is it for?: Everyone! Dogs too!

Belle Isle is a small 54-acre island in the city of Richmond, Virginia. Belle Island lies within the James River, and being owned by the city it serves as a city park.
